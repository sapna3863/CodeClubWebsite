About Code Club

Sapna Patel

Founder, Code Club

Hi, I'm Sapna Patel, the founder of Code Club at the Otto Bruyns Public Library and a high school student with a passion for technology and education. After coding at my local library since elementary school, I started Code Club to share my love for STEM and make coding accessible to middle and high school students from all backgrounds. Through engaging workshops, hands-on learning, and community events, I strive to inspire creativity, collaboration, and problem-solving in the next generation of innovators. I even coded this website myself!

Weekly Workshops

Hands-on sessions on programming, robotics, and web development. Learn to use current day computer chips (Microbits, Ardunio Uno, Jimu) and coding languages (HTML, CSS, Scratch, Javascript, Java, Microsoft MakeCode).

Community Engagement

For all students K - 12! All students from all different academic backgrounds welcome. Connected with over 1,400 individuals monthly through social media and grants.

Workshops

Our weekly workshops are interactive and engaging. We have workshops every Saturday at the Northfield library from 2:30 - 4:00 pm! Scroll below to see photos of our work and stay tuned for updates on upcoming sessions and project showcases!

What We Use

At Code Club, we provide students with the latest tools to help them learn and create. Here are some of the resources we use in our workshops:

Scratch

A beginner-friendly programming language where students can create interactive stories, games, and animations.

Microbits

A small, programmable computer used to teach students about electronics and coding through hands-on projects.

Jimu Robots

Robotics kits that allow students to build and program their own robots, encouraging problem-solving and engineering skills.

Microsoft MakeCode

A block-based programming platform that makes it easy for students to get started with coding and create their own interactive projects.

Arduino Uno

A popular open-source electronics platform that enables students to build everything from simple circuits to complex robotic systems.

JavaScript

One of the most widely used programming languages, essential for building websites and developing interactive content.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join?

All students from grades K–12 are welcome, regardless of experience level!

Do I need my own laptop?

If you can bring your own laptop it is appreciated. But if not, we have a limited number of desktops.

How much does it cost?

It’s completely free — thanks to library grants and community support.
